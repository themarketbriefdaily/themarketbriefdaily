Academic foundation

RS‑GAS‑t copula
with MIDAS drivers.

The bot is built on a regime‑switching, score‑driven Student‑t copula that captures time‑varying stock‑bond dependence. Two specifications separate medium‑horizon macro‑slow dynamics from daily asymmetric adjustments.

Model A · Medium horizon

Targets 5–63 day correlations using HAR‑style realised correlation drivers and MIDAS‑filtered macro factors (inflation, term spread, gold). The score update uses Fisher information scaling for stability. Regime‑specific ν and persistence capture calm versus stress states.

ρt+1 = ωs + βszt + αSt + δ′Λt

Model B · Daily asymmetric

Forecasts one‑day‑ahead dependence with a shrunk target (EWMA‑stabilised) and an augmented score that reacts more strongly to joint downside movements. No Fisher scaling — uses robust damping alone for faster adaptation.

St* = St + γabs|X| + γsign sign(X(s)X(b))

The transition probabilities between regimes are driven by the VIX (standardised via a quadratic logit). All parameters are estimated via penalised maximum likelihood on a training sample; no re‑estimation is done in the paper account. Full details in the working paper.

The macro momentum strategy.

The bot trades macro momentum across major asset classes using the dependence forecasts from the RS‑GAS‑t copula. When the model signals a regime change or an extreme correlation forecast, the bot adjusts its bias and triggers trades in equities, bonds, gold, dollar, or crude oil. Positions are held from hours to days, with pre‑defined stop losses (1.5× ATR) and take profits (2.5× ATR).
