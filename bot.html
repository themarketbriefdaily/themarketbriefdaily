Research foundation

Built on academic rigour.

The trading bot is the practical implementation of a dissertation that models time‑varying equity‑bond dependence using a regime‑switching score‑driven Student‑t copula with macro‑conditioned transitions.

"The correlation between equities and government bonds is a cornerstone of modern portfolio construction … however, this hedge is neither constant nor reliable. The sign and magnitude of stock‑bond correlations have shifted markedly across macroeconomic regimes."

Key contributions

  • First copula model to combine score‑driven updates, regime switching, and MIDAS‑filtered macro factors simultaneously.
  • Two complementary specifications: medium‑horizon (Model A) for strategic allocation, and daily asymmetric (Model B) for tactical risk control.
  • Empirically validated over 1990–2025 with robust out‑of‑sample portfolio benefits.

Why it matters

Static correlation models fail during crises. This bot adapts in real time to inflation shocks, flight‑to‑quality, and monetary policy shifts — exactly when traditional 60/40 portfolios break down.

Model design

RS‑GAS‑t copula
with MIDAS drivers.

Two specifications separate medium‑horizon macro‑slow dynamics from daily asymmetric adjustments, all within a unified probabilistic backbone.

Model A · Medium horizon

Targets 5–63 day correlations using HAR‑style realised correlation drivers and MIDAS‑filtered macro factors (inflation, term spread, gold). Score updates use Fisher scaling for stability.

ρt+1 = ωs + βszt + αSt + δ′Λt

Model B · Daily asymmetric

Forecasts one‑day‑ahead dependence with a shrunk target and asymmetric score that reacts more strongly to joint downside moves. Robust damping replaces Fisher scaling for speed.

St* = St + γabs|X| + γsign sign(X(s)X(b))
Core mathematics

Key equations
at a glance.

The structural backbone presented transparently — proprietary calibration details omitted.

Student‑t Copula Density

cν(u,v;ρ) = f2,ν(Fν−1(u), Fν−1(v);ρ) / [fν(Fν−1(u)) · fν(Fν−1(v))]

Bivariate Student‑t copula with heavy tails and non‑zero tail dependence — captures joint extremes.

Tail Dependence

λU = 2 Tν+1( −√[(ν+1)(1−ρ)/(1+ρ)] )

Upper tail dependence quantifies joint extreme positive returns. Lower ν increases λU.

GAS Update

zt+1 = ω + β zt + α St ,  St = ℐt−1/2 ∂ℓt/∂zt

Correlation evolves via the scaled score of the copula log‑likelihood — parameterisation‑invariant.

Time‑Varying Transitions

Pr(st+1=j|st=i, Zt) = exp{ηij+γ1Zt+γ2Zt2} / Σk exp{ηik+γ1Zt+γ2Zt2}

Regime transitions driven by quadratic logit in VIX — stress probabilities rise non‑linearly with volatility.
