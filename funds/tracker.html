05 / Strategy

The thesis
is humility.

The Tracker portfolio is built on a deceptively simple insight from Sharpe's 1964 CAPM: under the model's assumptions (homogeneous expectations, frictionless markets, single-period investment horizon), every investor optimally holds the market portfolio — a value-weighted basket of all risky assets — combined with the risk-free rate to match their personal risk tolerance.

The model's assumptions don't hold in reality, of course. But the empirical performance of the value-weighted market portfolio against more elaborate strategies remains remarkable: it survives the size, value, momentum, quality, and low-volatility factor literature largely intact at the aggregate level. Most active managers who try to beat it fail, net of fees, over any meaningful horizon.

This portfolio is for the investor who has concluded — correctly, in my view — that the market portfolio is a hard-to-beat baseline. It is the cheapest, simplest, most diversified expression of "be long the world."

  • No factor tilts. Size, value, momentum, quality — all left to the market.
  • No active picks. Sector / region / single-stock weights = market cap weights.
  • No tactical timing. Allocations are not changed in response to macro forecasts.
  • No alternatives. Real assets, commodities, hedge-fund replicators are excluded.
06 / Methodology

Three simple rules.

01

Equity / bond split by risk profile

Five variants — Cautious (30/70), Moderate (50/50), Balanced (65/35), Growth (80/20), Adventurous (95/5). The split is the only judgment in the portfolio.

02

Within each sleeve, market-cap weights

Equity sleeve: US equity at its global market-cap weight (~62%), developed ex-US (~24%), EM (~8%), with REIT (~3%) as a real-asset sliver. Bond sleeve: US Agg + Intl Agg in proportion to outstanding-issue size.

03

Annual rebalancing only

Rebalanced once per year (every January) to reset to target weights. Sub-yearly drift is tolerated to minimise transaction costs. No intra-year tactical changes ever.
