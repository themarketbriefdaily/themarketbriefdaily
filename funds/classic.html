05 / Strategy

The thesis.

The Classic portfolio is built on three findings from financial economics that have survived 50 years of empirical replication:

  • The equity risk premium exists. Long-run equity returns exceed the risk-free rate by ~5–6% per year, compensation for bearing undiversifiable market risk.
  • Small-cap stocks earn an additional premium (SMB). Smaller firms have historically delivered ~2–3% annualised excess return over large-caps, after adjusting for market exposure.
  • Value stocks earn a separate premium (HML). Firms with high book-to-market ratios (i.e. priced cheaply relative to their accounting book value) have historically outperformed growth stocks by ~3–4% annualised.

The portfolio captures these premia by overweighting small-cap value and large-cap value ETFs, complemented by a fixed-income sleeve for risk control. There is no active stock picking, no market timing, no sector views. The only judgments embedded in the strategy are the original Fama–French factor specifications — every allocation flows mechanically from those.

Crucially, this is not a strategy designed to outperform every quarter. Value has underperformed growth for extended periods (2009–2020 being the most extreme), and small-caps have multi-year stretches of disappointing returns. The portfolio is built for investors who can tolerate factor drawdowns of 12–18 months in exchange for the long-run premium.

06 / Methodology

Construction rules.

Every allocation decision is governed by a written rule. The portfolio is reconstructed quarterly using the latest factor data — no discretionary changes between rebalances.

01

Factor target weights

The portfolio targets a +0.35 SMB and +0.40 HML loading vs the MSCI World benchmark. Constituent ETF weights are solved as the optimisation that minimises tracking error subject to those loading constraints.

02

Rebalancing trigger

Quarterly check on calendar dates (end of Mar / Jun / Sep / Dec). Any holding that has drifted by more than ±300 bps from its target weight is reset; smaller drifts are tolerated to minimise transaction costs.

03

Universe screening

Only ETFs with AUM > USD 500M, 3-month average daily volume > USD 5M, and total expense ratio < 0.40% qualify for inclusion. Tracking-error vs the stated underlying index is reviewed annually.

04

Cash buffer

A 2–6% cash buffer is maintained at all times to absorb rebalancing flow, dividend reinvestment timing, and any subscription/redemption activity. Cash is held in SHV (iShares Short Treasury) for yield pickup.

05

Benchmark

MSCI World Net Total Return (USD). All performance comparisons net of estimated transaction costs (5 bps per round-trip trade) and gross of any management fee. The illustrative OCF of 0.22% reflects the weighted average of underlying ETF expense ratios.

06

Risk variants

The Classic strategy is available in five risk profiles — Cautious, Moderate, Balanced, Growth, Adventurous — which differ in the equity / fixed-income split (30/70 through 95/5). The factor tilts within the equity sleeve are constant across profiles.
