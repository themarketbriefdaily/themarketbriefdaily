03 / Entry signal

Three filters,
all must trigger.

A position is only opened when an upcoming earnings event in a liquid name passes three simultaneous gates. The filters are tightened from the Tastytrade volatility-crush playbook with adjustments for term-structure slope and Yang–Zhang realised volatility.

F1

Implied vs realised vol ratio

30-day at-the-money implied volatility divided by 30-day Yang–Zhang realised volatility must exceed 1.25. This indicates the option market is pricing in materially more future volatility than the stock has actually delivered — a necessary condition for vol-crush to be profitable.

F2

Term-structure slope

Linear slope of the ATM-IV term structure from DTE[0] to DTE=45 must be ≤ −0.00406. A negative slope (backwardation) means the front month is priced higher than further-out expirations — the market is event-pricing the earnings move and will revert post-print.

F3

Liquidity floor

30-day average daily share volume must exceed 1.5M. This guarantees tight option spreads and ability to exit at fair mid in the post-earnings window. Stocks below this threshold are skipped regardless of how rich the vol is.

04 / Position structure

Defined risk,
always.

No naked option positions are ever held. Every short-premium structure is paired with a long-option leg further out-of-the-money that caps the maximum loss before the trade is opened. The Greek profile and the maximum P&L are known at trade inception.

06 / Methodology

Construction rules.

01

Universe

Coverage is restricted to single-stock equity options with weekly expiries and an upcoming earnings event within 2–14 calendar days. Index options, ETF options, and commodity options are excluded.

02

Sizing rule

Maximum loss per trade is capped at 1% of portfolio NAV. Position notional is set so that the worst-case payout of the structure (e.g. the wing width of an iron condor minus credit received) equals that 1% threshold.

03

Strike selection

Short strikes are placed at the ±1σ level implied by the front-month ATM IV. Long wings are placed at ±2σ for iron condors. The credit received targets 20–30% of the max-loss capital.

04

Entry timing

Positions are opened between T-1 and T-3 trading days before the scheduled earnings release, when IV has typically richened to its event peak. Entry is skipped if the bid-ask spread on any leg exceeds 10% of the mid.

05

Exit rule

Positions are closed at open T+1 (the morning after earnings) regardless of P&L. No "letting it ride." If 80% of max profit is captured before earnings (rare), the position is closed early.

06

Concentration limits

No more than 3 positions in the same sector, no more than 2 positions with overlapping earnings dates, and total book vega exposure capped at −$2,500 per $100k NAV.
