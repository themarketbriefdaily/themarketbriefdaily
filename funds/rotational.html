05 / Strategy

Four theses.

Theme 1 — Precious metals (19%). A structural hedge against fiscal indiscipline, currency debasement, and a regime of higher trend inflation than the 2010s. Gold and silver have historically delivered positive real returns during periods of negative real rates — exactly the regime central banks are signalling they will tolerate. Sizing reflects the asymmetric tail-risk hedge value rather than expected total return.

Theme 2 — Clean energy transition (11%). The single largest capital-formation cycle in industrial history, mandated by policy and increasingly economic without subsidy. ICLN provides diversified exposure across solar, wind, energy storage, and grid infrastructure. Position size is moderate to reflect the cyclicality of the sub-sector and historical drawdown profile (~50% peak-to-trough in 2021–23).

Theme 3 — European defensive staples (10%). A counterweight to the growth-heavy AI / cyber exposure. European consumer-staples companies (Nestlé, Unilever, Diageo, Pernod, L'Oréal) trade at structurally lower multiples than US peers, generate stable cash flows, and benefit from cyclical EUR weakness. IECS provides the cleanest passive expression of that view.

Theme 4 — AI / cyber / quantum (42%). The largest single theme, expressed through six positions. The thesis is that compute, security, and edge networking are the four picks-and-shovels of the AI build-out. NVDA is the dominant compute hardware. CRWD / FTNT / PANW are the three structurally dominant cyber platforms. NET is the edge / serverless infrastructure. QTUM provides early-stage exposure to quantum without single-name concentration risk. MKSI provides exposure to the semiconductor capital-equipment cycle.

06 / Methodology

How it is managed.

01

Theme weights are the primary lever

Theme allocations are set quarterly. Within each theme, position weights are allowed to drift up to ±200 bps from target before triggering a rebalance.

02

Rotation rules

Theme weights are reviewed against three macro signals: real-rate regime (TIPS yield), USD trend (DXY 200-day), and credit conditions (HY OAS). A material shift in any two of three triggers a theme-level re-weighting at the next quarter-end.

03

Single-name caps

No single stock may exceed 15% of the portfolio. Theme caps: cybersecurity ≤ 25%, AI infrastructure ≤ 25%, precious metals ≤ 25%, all other themes ≤ 20%. Concentration limits are checked at every rebalance.

04

Quality screen

Single-name positions must meet liquidity (ADV > USD 200M), profitability (positive trailing FCF), and balance-sheet (net debt / EBITDA < 4x) thresholds. ETFs must have AUM > USD 200M and tracking error < 150 bps vs index.

05

Drawdown protocol

At a portfolio drawdown of −10% from peak, the cash buffer is increased from 4% to 8% and rebalancing is deferred to the next scheduled date. At −15% drawdown, theme weights are reset to neutral and the rotation framework is reviewed.

06

Benchmark

MSCI World Net Total Return (USD). The fund's tracking error is intentionally high (~12%) reflecting the concentrated thematic nature. Benchmark comparison is informational; the fund is not managed to outperform any single index on any single quarter.
