Investment Philosophy

How we think about markets

Three pillars underpin every analysis and fund strategy we publish.

📊

Market Structure First

We begin with order flow dynamics, liquidity patterns, and microstructure effects before drawing macro conclusions. Understanding the plumbing is prerequisite to understanding price.

⚙️

Physical Constraints Matter

Commodity supply bottlenecks, production capacity, and delivery dynamics are leading indicators — not lagging narratives. We track physical tightness signals ahead of financial market acknowledgment.

🔗

Transmission Mechanics

Policy signals, supply shocks, and financial conditions all propagate through connected markets in predictable sequences. Mapping these transmission channels gives us structural edge.

Our Approach

Rigorous, independent, actionable

No prediction theater. No hype.

Every analysis published by The Market Brief Daily is grounded in defensible frameworks and real-world market mechanics — not consensus or ideology. We focus on what changed, why it matters, and what to watch next.

Our research combines quantitative rigor with qualitative judgment. When our framework is wrong, we say so explicitly and update our view. Intellectual honesty is a competitive advantage.

  • Metals market plumbing — COMEX inventory, delivery dynamics, and physical tightness signals
  • Macro transmission — how rates, FX, and liquidity propagate into commodities and risk assets
  • Risk framing — scenarios, invalidation conditions, and indicators to monitor
  • Portfolio construction — multi-asset allocation insights for regime-aware strategies
  • Factor premia — systematic identification of persistent return sources across market cycles

Daily Data Refresh

All fund performance metrics, benchmark comparisons, and risk statistics are calculated daily after US market close using live price data.

Benchmark-Relative Performance

Every fund is evaluated against its primary benchmark (SPY or ACWX) using Jensen's Alpha, Sharpe Ratio, Sortino Ratio, and Max Drawdown metrics.
