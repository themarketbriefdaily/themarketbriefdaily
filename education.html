What this section covers

  • Unit 1: investment environment foundations (markets, ethics, regulation, legal basics, advice logic, taxation).
  • Unit 2: investment practice workflows (applied ethics, conduct workflows, suitability pathways, financial-crime awareness, portfolio implementation, documentation logic).
  • Chapter-by-chapter learning outcomes, practical checklists, scenarios, exam tips, and knowledge checks.

How to use it

  • Read chapters in order first, then use glossary tooltips for revision loops.
  • Use chapter checklists to convert concepts into actions.
  • Attempt question accordions before opening model answers and rationale.
  • Use the Unit 1-to-Unit 2 bridge notes to avoid fragmented understanding.

Study path options

Beginner path

Complete Unit 1 in sequence, prioritize glossary terms and practical checklists, then enter Unit 2 once Unit 1 chapter checks are stable.

Exam path

Follow chapter order, then run question-then-answer cycles chapter by chapter, emphasizing “why alternatives are wrong.”

Practitioner path

Use “In practice” and case-study blocks first, then backfill theory to strengthen decision quality and documentation discipline.
