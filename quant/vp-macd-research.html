Why each VP-MACD component is there

Volume-price

VP-MACD adjustment

The trend signal uses a volume-price-adjusted close: P_adj = Close × (1 + α·Z_vol) / (1 + β·(vol_ratio−1)). High-volume moves are amplified (confirmation), high short-term-vol periods are dampened (stress). All volume inputs are lagged by 1 day to prevent look-ahead. Filters low-volume false breakouts that raw price would catch.

Diversifiers

Min-variance GLD/TLT

The v5c trend gate (200d MA) kicked GLD/TLT out exactly when they were most needed — during equity drawdowns, GLD/TLT often fall below their 200d MA as part of the dislocation, then rebound as hedges. VP-MACD uses a closed-form 2-asset min-variance split with no trend gate, keeping diversifiers in at all times.

Costs

Spread-aware hysteresis

Per-sleeve hysteresis bands calibrated to each instrument's bid-ask spread: tighter on liquid sleeves (VUSA.L), wider on the 3x leveraged ETF (3USL.L). A turnover cap prevents >100% daily turnover in stress windows. The cost model charges the round-trip half-spread on each delta.

Validation

2019–2020 OOS lock

The VP-MACD α (0.10) and β (0.02) were optimised on 2019–2020 out-of-sample data, then locked. No parameter has been changed since. This is the same discipline as v5c's pre-registered ablation — the configuration that cleared validation is the one that ships.

Regime gate

Unchanged from v5c

VIX3M>VIX contango + 210d SMA trend, 3-month hysteresis. The VP adjustment feeds into the trend filter (via the adjusted price) but the regime logic itself is identical to v5c. Credit gate (HYG < 200d MA) and total-portfolio vol-cap @ 28% are also unchanged.

Risk control

Daily de-risk overlay

A fast median-vol / realised-vol scalar on the equity sleeves, unchanged from v5c. Reacts intraday-to-month faster than the monthly regime gate, trimming exposure as volatility spikes. The VP adjustment makes this more accurate by filtering the vol estimate.
