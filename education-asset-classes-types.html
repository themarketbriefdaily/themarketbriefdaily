Asset Classes & Asset Types

Economic role, payoff structure, valuation methods, and risk regimes across major investable instruments.

1. Equity claims and valuation anchors

Residual claims, growth assumptions, and discount-rate sensitivity.

Learning outcomes

  • Relate earnings/cash flows to valuation regimes.
  • Assess margin and duration-of-growth risk.
  • Distinguish cyclicals vs defensives in macro states.

2. Sovereign fixed income and duration

Policy-rate expectations, term premia, and convexity effects.

Learning outcomes

  • Interpret duration as first-order rate sensitivity.
  • Evaluate curve positioning decisions.
  • Integrate rate path uncertainty into allocations.

3. Credit spreads and default risk

Spread decomposition: expected loss, liquidity, and risk appetite.

Learning outcomes

  • Separate duration vs spread contributions.
  • Assess downgrade and default cycle risk.
  • Use spread levels in regime-context, not isolation.

4. Inflation-linked assets

Linkers, real yields, break-even inflation, and inflation hedge quality.

Learning outcomes

  • Interpret real vs nominal yield shifts.
  • Use break-even inflation for macro diagnostics.
  • Assess inflation-hedge implementation trade-offs.

5. Real assets and alternatives

Property, infrastructure, private markets, and valuation smoothing issues.

Learning outcomes

  • Explain illiquidity premium arguments critically.
  • Identify appraisal smoothing and stale-pricing risk.
  • Compare listed vs private implementation routes.

6. Commodities and futures curves

Spot return, collateral return, roll yield, and inventory signals.

Learning outcomes

  • Explain contango/backwardation effects.
  • Connect commodity dynamics to inflation regimes.
  • Avoid simplistic spot-price extrapolation.

7. FX as macro relative-value exposure

Carry, valuation, policy divergence, and risk-off dynamics.

Learning outcomes

  • Interpret FX exposure as relative macro bet.
  • Identify funding-currency and risk sentiment channels.
  • Evaluate hedged vs unhedged foreign allocations.

8. Derivatives and synthetic exposure

Options, forwards, swaps, and leverage/hedging design.

Learning outcomes

  • Map nonlinear payoff structures.
  • Assess collateral and margin requirements.
  • Control basis and counterparty risk.

9. Liquidity tiers and instrument selection

Instrument choice by trading cost, depth, and use-case.

Learning outcomes

  • Match instruments to horizon and liquidity needs.
  • Estimate implementation risk by market tier.
  • Prevent hidden complexity in retail implementation.

10. Cross-asset portfolio interactions

Correlation instability, tail co-movement, and regime diversification.

Learning outcomes

  • Stress test diversification assumptions.
  • Understand when bonds hedge equities and when not.
  • Build robust multi-asset risk budgets.

Core references

  • CFA asset class and portfolio readings.
  • Fama-French and multifactor literature.
  • BIS cross-asset and liquidity publications.
  • Major index provider methodology papers.
Educational content; check product-level documentation before investing in complex instruments.
