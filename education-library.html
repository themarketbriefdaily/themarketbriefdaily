Active vs passive in 2024: arithmetic of costs and persistence

SPIVA UK equity underperformance (72%) and Morningstar global active manager underperformance (~91%) interpreted through graduate-level portfolio theory.

MSc Core
  • SPIVA 2024 UK scorecards report ~72% active UK equity underperformance.
  • Morningstar long-horizon manager barometers indicate ~91% global equity active manager underperformance in key cohorts.
  • Sharpe arithmetic + implementation costs explain persistent median active shortfall.

In aggregate, active investors are the market portfolio before costs; therefore, after costs they must lag by the weighted cost differential. The empirical problem is not proving that skilled managers exist, but identifying them ex ante with robustness to style drift, closures, and regime shifts. This is where SPIVA persistence and survivorship methodologies materially improve inference quality.

When survivorship bias, transaction costs, and fee compounding are modelled correctly, net alpha hurdles become materially higher than headline active outperformance tables imply.

Morningstar/SPIVA dedicated section: long-horizon persistence and survivorship

UK 72% active underperformance, global 91%, persistence failure, and cost decomposition across 10+ years.

Evidence
  • 2024 UK equity underperformance: 72% (SPIVA).
  • Global equity active manager underperformance: ~91% in long-horizon Morningstar cohorts.
  • Persistence decay: top-quartile winners often fail to persist over 5-10 year follow-up windows.

Survivorship controls matter because dead funds contain adverse information about process fragility and fee burden. Excluding those funds inflates apparent manager skill and creates a biased prior for allocator decisions. This is especially relevant in retail UK platforms where headline returns are often shown on surviving products.
