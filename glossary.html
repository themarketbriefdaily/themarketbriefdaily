Investment glossary

Use the live search and A–Z index to quickly decode technical terms used across our market briefings.

ABCDEFGHIJKLMNOPQRSTUVWXYZ

A

Alpha

Return generated above a benchmark after adjusting for comparable risk.

B

Basis trade

Relative-value position targeting convergence between cash and derivative pricing.

C

Carry

Income earned from holding an asset independent of spot price appreciation.

D

Duration

Sensitivity of a bond price to changes in interest rates.

E

Earnings revision breadth

Share of analyst estimate revisions that are positive across a coverage universe.

F

Forward curve

Market-implied pricing of an asset or rate across future maturities.

G

Gamma

Rate of change in an option’s delta as the underlying price moves.

H

Hedge ratio

Proportion of exposure offset with a hedging instrument.

I

Implied volatility

Market expectation of future volatility inferred from option prices.

J

J-curve effect

Pattern where near-term performance dips before improving in a longer horizon.

K

Key rate duration

Sensitivity to shifts at specific points of the yield curve.

L

Liquidity premium

Additional expected return for holding less liquid assets.

M

Macro regime

Broad economic environment defined by growth, inflation, and policy dynamics.

N

Net exposure

Directional exposure after offsetting long and short positions.

O

Order-book depth

Amount of executable liquidity available at nearby price levels.

P

Policy rate corridor

Upper and lower bounds for short-term funding rates set by a central bank.

Q

Quality factor

Equity characteristic linked to balance-sheet strength and earnings durability.

R

Risk parity

Portfolio approach balancing risk contribution rather than capital weights.

S

Sharpe ratio

Excess return earned per unit of realised volatility.

T

Term premium

Compensation investors require for holding long-dated bonds.

U

Upside capture

Degree to which a strategy participates in benchmark gains.

V

Value at Risk

Statistical estimate of potential loss at a given confidence interval.

W

Weighted average maturity

Average time to maturity of holdings weighted by position size.

X

Ex-ante risk

Forecast portfolio risk based on modelled covariance and exposures.

Y

Yield curve steepening

Increase in long-end yields relative to short-end yields.

Z

Zero-coupon yield

Discount-implied yield from a bond cash flow stripped of coupons.
