Methodology

How the briefs and model funds are built

The frameworks, data sources, and rules behind every publication. Updated when the methodology changes — never silently.

Last updated May 6, 2026 v1.4

1. Analytical framework

Every brief is structured around three lenses, applied in order:

  • Market structure first. Order flow, dealer positioning, options gamma, and microstructure plumbing before drawing macro conclusions. Understanding how price gets made is prerequisite to understanding why it moved.
  • Physical constraints next. Inventory, delivery, freight, and production capacity are leading signals — not lagging narratives.
  • Macro transmission last. Policy signals, financial conditions, and supply shocks propagate through connected markets in identifiable sequences. The job is to map the channel before the price has moved.

If a brief skips a lens, it says so explicitly.

2. Data sources

All sources are public, vendor-licensed, or reproducible. No paid signals. No proprietary feeds.

FRED (St. Louis Fed) U.S. Treasury / TreasuryDirect BLS / BEA COMEX / CME public reports USDA WASDE EIA weekly inventory Bank of England / ECB / BOJ SEC EDGAR (13F, 10-Q) OCC equity options data Yahoo Finance / public price feeds

Charts in briefs cite their underlying source in the caption. Where a calculation is non-trivial, the formula is shown inline.

3. Model fund construction

The four model portfolios shown on /funds are illustrative — built to demonstrate frameworks, not to track a real account.

Allocation rules

  • Universe. Liquid public ETFs and futures-linked instruments only. No single-name equity, no OTC, no levered products beyond 2×.
  • Rebalancing. Monthly, on the first business day. No mid-month adjustments unless a regime threshold is breached.
  • Risk targeting. Each fund targets an annualised volatility band; weights are scaled to keep ex-ante vol inside it.
  • No look-ahead. Allocations at any historical date use only data available before that date's close.
Illustrative only. Returns shown for these portfolios are simulated and do not include real trading frictions (slippage, taxes, financing). They are useful for relative comparison and framework demonstration — not as a guide to live performance.

4. Performance calculation

  • Total return. Daily returns chained geometrically; dividends reinvested at close. Index base = 100.
  • Benchmark. Each fund is paired with a single, fixed benchmark (e.g. SPY, ACWX, AGG). Excess return is fund − benchmark, both calculated on the same calendar.
  • Risk metrics. Sharpe (rf = 3-month T-bill), Sortino (downside-only deviation), max drawdown (peak-to-trough). Annualised using √252.
  • Reporting cadence. Snapshot regenerated nightly after US market close.

5. Editorial rules

  • No paid promotions, sponsorships, or affiliate links inside research.
  • No undisclosed positions in single-name securities discussed in a brief.
  • If a prior view is wrong, it gets a correction in the next brief — labelled as such.
  • Forecasts are stated with explicit invalidation conditions, never as standalone targets.
  • AI assistance is used for editing and code, not for writing analysis.
