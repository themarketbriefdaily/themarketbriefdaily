Investing

From objective setting to portfolio governance, with emphasis on net outcomes after fees, taxes, and behaviour.

Behavioural risk control8. Scenario and stress testing9. Performance attribution10. Governance and monitoring

1. Investor objectives and constraints

Return targets, risk tolerance, horizon, liquidity, legal and tax constraints.

Learning outcomes

  • Define investable objectives in real terms.
  • Separate risk capacity from risk appetite.
  • Translate constraints into policy parameters.

2. Strategic asset allocation

Policy mix as primary long-run driver of outcome distribution.

Learning outcomes

  • Build strategic allocations from economic exposures.
  • Evaluate concentration and correlation instability risk.
  • Set tolerance bands and capital-deployment rules.

3. Active vs passive arithmetic

Sharpe framework and manager-selection base-rate reality.

Learning outcomes

  • Explain why aggregate active underperforms net of costs.
  • Assess edge requirements for selective active mandates.
  • Design core-satellite implementation choices.

4. Fees, turnover, and tax drag

Compounding impact of controllable frictions.

Learning outcomes

  • Model net return decomposition from gross assumptions.
  • Prioritize certainty-adjusted alpha from cost control.
  • Identify avoidable implementation leakage.

5. Factor investing and robustness

Value, size, momentum, quality, and multifactor portfolio integration.

Learning outcomes

  • Interpret factor premia as noisy and regime-dependent.
  • Manage factor crowding and turnover risk.
  • Use multifactor blending to improve resilience.

6. Rebalancing policy design

Calendar, threshold, and hybrid methods across wrappers and taxable accounts.

Learning outcomes

  • Balance risk control against transaction costs.
  • Document trigger and exception rules.
  • Prevent drift-driven hidden risk concentration.

7. Behavioural risk control

Process design to mitigate panic selling, recency bias, and overtrading.

Learning outcomes

  • Implement pre-commitment decision rules.
  • Use dashboards over narratives in volatile regimes.
  • Reduce behavioural drawdown amplification.

8. Scenario and stress testing

Inflation shocks, growth shocks, duration shocks, and liquidity freezes.

Learning outcomes

  • Run scenario-based robustness checks.
  • Identify hidden beta bundles.
  • Plan policy actions ahead of stress periods.

9. Performance attribution

Asset allocation, security selection, timing, and implementation effects.

Learning outcomes

  • Separate luck from process quality.
  • Benchmark appropriately by mandate.
  • Evaluate net-of-fee persistent skill claims.

10. Governance and monitoring

KPI cadence, review discipline, and strategy-continuity controls.

Learning outcomes

  • Set monthly, quarterly, annual governance workflows.
  • Detect thesis drift and implementation slippage.
  • Align monitoring with long-horizon goals.

Core references

  • Sharpe (1991), The Arithmetic of Active Management.
  • SPIVA scorecards and persistence studies.
  • CFA portfolio management and ethics curriculum.
  • Vanguard and Dimensional implementation research.
  • Fama (1970), Markowitz (1952), Fama-French research stream.
Educational content; tailor implementation to personal circumstances and regulated advice where needed.
